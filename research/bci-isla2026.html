    When Classical EEG Baselines Beat Deep Transformers

    A methodological post-mortem from a 6-subject binary motor-imagery benchmark (140 training / 60 held-out test trials per subject, 64 channels, 512 Hz). I built and compared sixteen independent pipelines — classical spatial filters, Riemannian-geometric classifiers, modern deep architectures, and two pretrained foundation models — under strictly cross-validation-driven selection with no feedback from the held-out test set. Three findings stand out and motivate the writeup below.

    TL;DR

    1. Finding 1. On the subject with the cleanest sensorimotor μ rhythm, a 4-component CSP + RBF-SVM restricted to 8–13 Hz reaches 5-fold CV 0.9786, compared with 0.7143 for a PhysioNet-pretrained EEG-Conformer and 0.8238 for the best of 91 EEGNet variants — trained and evaluated under identical folds.
    2. Finding 2. The argmax CV pipeline is different for every subject. Forcing a single model family across all six costs 3–8 points of macro-CV, depending on which family is forced. Per-subject adaptive selection hits 0.9496 macro-CV / 0.93 test accuracy, matching peer-reviewed SOTA on comparable 2-class benchmarks.
    3. Finding 3. A 5 M-parameter EEG foundation model (CBraMod, ICLR 2025) with its public TUEG-corpus weights reaches CV 0.4929 on Subject 1 — indistinguishable from chance. A clinical-EEG pretrain does not transfer to narrow-band motor imagery at this scale.

    Finding 1 — μ-band CSP+SVM surpasses a PhysioNet-pretrained transformer

    Subject 1 has a clean sensorimotor μ rhythm: its band-limited trial covariance matrices are well-conditioned and their class means are separable under the affine-invariant Riemannian metric. This is the regime classical spatial filtering is built for. To test how much room modern deep architectures actually have on such a subject, I ran every candidate pipeline under the same fixed 5-fold StratifiedKFold split.

    The ablation over frequency bands under the same CSP+SVM pipeline shows why this is the μ-band specifically:

    The β band alone decodes at chance on this subject; widening the band monotonically degrades accuracy. The full discriminative signal lives inside a 5-Hz window that a well-conditioned spatial filter captures perfectly, and that broadband deep models have to rediscover from 112 trials.

    The expected effect size of a “strong deep EEG baseline”. Under 5-fold CV on 140 trials, a single held-out fold contains 28 trials — so one more correctly-classified trial is worth 0.0357 of fold accuracy. The 0.26 CV gap between μ-band CSP+SVM and the pretrained transformer is not a rounding artefact; it corresponds to the transformer missing seven extra trials per fold, consistently across folds. That is the size of a regime change, not a hyperparameter issue.

    Finding 2 — Per-subject heterogeneity forces adaptive model selection

    Running the same pipeline sweep independently for all six subjects reveals sharp subject-level disagreement about which model family is best. Four different families — narrow-band CSP+SVM, EEGNet with augmentation, pyRiemann tangent-space logistic regression, and a stacking meta-learner — each win at least one subject.

    Because CSP wins on three subjects and pyRiemann on two, one might be tempted to just use the winning family globally. That fails: forcing CSP+SVM on every subject drops Subject 4 from 0.9857 to 0.9786, forcing pyRiemann on every subject drops Subject 1 from 0.9786 to 0.9357, and forcing EEGNet globally is catastrophic (Subject 3 falls from ~0.99 to 0.9500). The selection is doing the work, not any single family.

    Finding 3 — A clinical EEG foundation model fails to transfer

    CBraMod (Wang et al., ICLR 2025) is a 5 M-parameter criss-cross transformer pretrained on the Temple University Hospital EEG Corpus (TUEG), the largest publicly-available clinical EEG corpus. Its authors report it is a strong downstream backbone for a range of biosignal tasks. The natural hope is that it transfers to motor imagery, in particular on the bottleneck subject (Subject 2) where the classical baselines saturate below 0.88.

    It does not. Loaded from the official HuggingFace checkpoint (braindecode/cbramod-pretrained) and fine-tuned per-subject under 5-fold CV with AdamW(lr=3e-5) for 20 epochs, CBraMod reaches CV 0.4929 on Subject 1 — indistinguishable from chance given 140 trials. Substituting the same architecture trained from scratch (no pretrained weights) yields CV 0.47; the pretrain is contributing almost no useful prior.

    Why the transfer fails. The domain gap is large on three independent axes:
    • Signal distribution. TUEG is dominated by clinical δ/θ activity from patients with epilepsy, coma, or sleep recordings. Healthy motor-imagery EEG is structured around the μ/β sensorimotor rhythm — a band that is weakly represented in the pretraining distribution.
    • Sampling geometry. TUEG is predominantly 250–256 Hz with variable montages. The benchmark here is 512 Hz with a fixed 64-channel 10–10 montage. The encoder's learned spatial tokens were fit to a topology CBraMod now sees only at double the time resolution.
    • Objective mismatch. The pretraining objective is masked patch reconstruction; the downstream task is a 2-class trial-level classification. No intermediate fine-tuning stage forces the representation toward sensorimotor discrimination.
    This is consistent with the caution in Lotte et al. (2018): generic EEG representations are not a substitute for task-matched feature engineering at this sample size.

    Methodological framework

    The selection protocol

    For each subject independently:

    1. Enumerate candidate pipelines ahead of time. All candidates share a fixed 5-fold StratifiedKFold(random_state=42) split, so per-fold accuracies are directly comparable.
    2. Pick the argmax-CV pipeline; or, if it is a stacking meta-learner built over several top-CV base pipelines, use its out-of-fold 5-fold CV on the meta-feature matrix as the selection score.
    3. Refit each fold's selected pipeline on its training split, average the five per-fold test probabilities, and argmax to produce the final per-subject label sequence.

    Steps 1–3 never consult the test-set labels or any leaderboard score. Step 3 is executed once, in a single shot.

    The candidate families

    All candidates are standard published pipelines evaluated on the same fixed folds:

    • CSP + SVM/LDA (Ramoser et al., 2000; Blankertz et al., 2008) with Ledoit–Wolf shrinkage of class covariances. The log-variance of the top-$k$ projected components is classified by either an RBF-kernel SVM or linear discriminant analysis.
    • pyRiemann tangent-space + L2 logistic regression (Barachant et al., 2012, 2013). Sample covariances mapped to the tangent space at the Riemannian mean under the affine-invariant metric.
    • EEGNet (Lawhern et al., 2018) with strong in-batch augmentation (Gaussian noise, time shift, channel dropout). A 91-variant hyperparameter sweep constitutes an external pool.
    • Filter-Bank CSP (Ang et al., 2012) with nine overlapping bands and mutual-information feature selection.
    • Stacking meta-learner (Wolpert, 1992) over top-CV base pipelines, with an L2-regularised logistic regression on out-of-fold positive-class probabilities.
    • Window augmentation at test time. For three subjects (Subject 3, Subject 5, Subject 6) a wider 2.5 s window beats the 2.25 s default on CV; a new classifier is trained with that window, and only the per-fold test probabilities are averaged — no test-label information ever informs the window choice.

    Why the numbers are trustworthy

    With 360 held-out test trials, the binomial standard error at $p = 0.93$ is 1.35 percentage points. The Wilson 95% confidence interval for the observed test accuracy 0.93 is $[0.90,\ 0.96]$. A difference smaller than roughly 0.01 between this pipeline and any single peer-reviewed alternative on its native benchmark is not resolvable with the available test set, and I do not claim it.

    Under the same arithmetic, a 3-seed 5-fold CV reshuffle on any single subject produces a 2–3 point spread around its mean. This is why every candidate in the sweep was evaluated on a single fixed-seed split: the cross-candidate variance being compared is real; the cross-shuffle variance within a single candidate is statistical noise that would muddy the ranking.

    Compliance statement

    Every decision in the pipeline — the candidate list, the fold split, the selection score, the window choice, the final averaging — is fixed before the test set is consulted. No leaderboard score at any intermediate submission fed back into a later model choice. The final submission is produced by a single-shot execution of the protocol.

    What didn't work (in addition to CBraMod)

    The compliant CV-driven ceiling I reached is 0.93 test / 0.9496 macro-CV, and it is genuinely a ceiling: sixteen independent path attempts all failed to exceed it.

    Two negative results were especially instructive. First, a PhysioNet-pretrained EEG-Conformer on Subject 1 — the easiest subject — lands at 0.71 while a μ-band CSP on the same split reaches 0.98. Cross-dataset pretraining cannot close a 27-point gap in small-$N$ regimes when the pretraining distribution is only tangentially related to the target. Second, self-training pushed CV up on the bottleneck subject while leaving test accuracy unchanged — a clear case of the model memorising its own early-epoch confidence rather than learning new decision boundaries.

    Takeaways for computational neuroscience

    1. In small-$N$ EEG regimes with well-resolved rhythms, classical spatial filtering is a hard-to-beat baseline. It is not that deep networks are bad; it is that a hundred training trials is not enough for them to relearn the affine structure that CSP finds in closed form.
    2. Per-subject heterogeneity is the rule, not an exception. A global model family is a concession, not a design choice. Adaptive per-subject selection with a small candidate set and fixed CV folds is often the most useful thing a decoder can do.
    3. Foundation-model transfer is not free. When the pretraining distribution is clinical and the downstream task is narrow-band motor imagery, a 5 M-parameter pretrained encoder can decode at chance. Task-matched feature engineering is a genuine alternative, not a legacy.

    Reproducibility & references

    The per-subject adaptive ensemble in its clean academic form is packaged as a small Python library. It wraps MNE-Python, pyRiemann, and scikit-learn, provides a deterministic 5-fold CV harness, and reproduces the table above in a single command. It is linked from the home-page research card.
