    George B. Moody PhysioNet Challenge 2026

    Decoding Cognitive Impairment from Overnight Polysomnography

    A research note (in progress) on decoding cognitive impairment from overnight polysomnography (PSG). Will cover nonlinear EEG dynamics (recurrence quantification, multiscale and permutation entropy) stratified across N2, N3, and REM sleep; brain and heart coupling between HRV and EEG delta-band activity; slow-wave dissipation and REM-sleep muscle atonia as biomarkers of neurodegenerative decline; and integration of pretrained EEG foundation-model embeddings (BIOT, REVE) within a cross-site leak-free ensemble. PhysioNet challenge organized by Gari Clifford (Emory / Georgia Tech).

    Why this page is sparse — and why the code repository is private.

    The George B. Moody PhysioNet Challenge 2026 is still in its active submission phase (Feb to Aug 2026). Per the challenge's sporting-conduct norms, both the detailed methodology writeup and the source-code repository are kept private until the official phase closes, so that ideas, feature definitions, and engineering decisions aren't inadvertently shared across teams mid-competition. The live leaderboard remains publicly viewable.

    The methodological note below will be filled in after the official phase concludes (expected Aug 2026), at which point the source repository will also be made public. The topic outline is included here as a preview of what the completed writeup will cover.

    Content pending. Will cover the PSG recording setup (multi-channel EEG, EKG, EMG, EOG, respiration), the challenge's prediction target, and the clinical motivation for overnight-sleep biomarkers of cognitive decline.

    2. Sleep Architecture & Why It Matters for Cognition

    Content pending. Will cover N2 (spindles, K-complexes), N3 (slow-wave sleep, memory consolidation, glymphatic clearance), REM (dreaming, synaptic homeostasis), and how each stage's neurophysiology relates to cognitive function.

    Content pending. Will cover Recurrence Quantification Analysis on phase-space embeddings, Multiscale Entropy slopes, and Permutation Entropy as measures of cortical complexity that degrade with neurodegeneration.

    4. Spectral & Slow-Wave Features

    Content pending. Will cover spectral edge frequency, band-power ratios, and slow-wave dissipation dynamics across the night.

    5. Brain–Heart Coupling

    Content pending. Will cover HRV↔EEG delta-band correlations during N3, LF-HRV↔delta during REM, wake-decoupling, and hemispheric asymmetry as cross-system coupling markers.

    Content pending. Will cover REM Sleep without Atonia (RSWA), its link to synucleinopathies (Parkinson's disease, Lewy body dementia), and quantification via REM/N2 EMG RMS ratios.
